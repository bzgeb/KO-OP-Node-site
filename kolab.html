INFO

KOLAB is an initiative by KO_OP & Andrew Nissen to fund the development of small games. Gamemakers are given 5000$ USD to make a game within a one to three month period. The goal is to provide game-makers outside the mainstream a small but significant opportunity to realize the games they want to make, without any mandatory repayment terms. Our hope is that KOLAB inspires other insitutions and models to enrich the ecosystem for #altgames to become more sustainable. KOLAB partners are discovered through a mix of submissions and invitations.

FAQ

  • How much is the fund and how is it paid out?
    - The fund is 5000$ USD and is paid half at the start and half at the completion of the game.
  • What kind of games are you looking for?
    - We're open to almost any kind of game that can be made within a 1-4 month period. If you check out our games you'll see we have a heavy emphasis on visuals but we encourage all forms including things like twine based games.
  • Do I own the game I make?
    - Absolutely. You own everything that you create, and you license it to KO_OP so that we can talk about it, post it on our games list, and otherwise help spread the word.
  • What's your cut from the game's sales?
    - That's up to you. If you feel like splitting a percentage with KO_OP we would gladly accept and reinvest the money for future KOLABS, but if making this game and its income are critical to your survival then we're committed to providing you with 100% of all the money you make. All we really require is a credit.
  • Can I make a game to be released for free?
    - Yes.
  • How many projects are you going to fund?
    - Right now we're committing to funding at least one project in 2016.
  • Can I submit a project already in development?
    - It depends. If you thin the project will be completed within a few months then sure. Our ideal candidate is making a small game that can be made from start to finish within the 1-4 month period however.
  • Will you sign an NDA?
    Nope. We respect people's confeditality and will never share any private details from game submissions, but we dont' believe it's necessary or practical to sign NDAs.
